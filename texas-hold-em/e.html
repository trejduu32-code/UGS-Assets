How to play Texas Hold'em

Do you love playing cards, especially poker? Try your hand at our free poker game online. At the start of the game, the dealer deals 2 cards to each player that only they can hold and see. Then, 5 cards are placed in the center of the table for everyone to use. These community cards are revealed in three stages:

  • Flop – The dealer flips the first 3 cards after the first betting round
  • Turn – One more card is added after a betting round
  • River – The final card is revealed after the final betting round

Your goal? Create the best hand using your cards and the ones in the middle to win the round!

Not sure what beats what? Check out the in-game Texas Hold 'Em hand rankings guide to master the poker hands! The better your hand, the better your chances of winning the round and, eventually, the game! Make sure you think through the betting round. These are your golden words for the best hand in Texas Holdem Poker: Bet, hold, check, call, raise, fold, and all-in.

The Action in Texas Hold'em Poker

Each round of free poker games follow this structure:

  • Players bet chips to stay in the game.
  • The first two players post a big blind and a small blind. The cards do not flop. This is regardless of the cards in hand.
  • You can call (match the highest bet), raise (increase the bet), or fold (drop out of the game). This is the most important part of the Poker game.
  • If nobody raises, players can check (stay in without betting more).
  • Go all in if you’re feeling bold!

Feeling lucky? The key to winning free poker games is knowing when to hold and when to fold! If you think your hand is strong, go for a raise and put pressure on your opponents. If the cards on the table aren't in your favor, it's sometimes best to fold and wait for a better round.


Play Free Poker Like a Pro

  • Know your hand – A strong hand increases your chances of winning poker.
  • Read the table – What cards are showing? Who is betting aggressively? This is key in the game.
  • Bluff smartly – Sometimes, a well-timed raise can make your opponents fold even if they have good cards in hand.
  • Watch the dealer – The dealer plays a crucial role in how the game unfolds, so pay attention!
  • Pay attention to the flop – The cards on the flop give you key information about possibilities with the cards in your hand. When in doubt, hold your bets and play poker like a pro!
